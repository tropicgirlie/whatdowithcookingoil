# Why Italy has CONOE and Ireland does not

Research note from 2026-05-18, parked for future product use.

## Short version

Italy built CONOE because three things lined up: an existing statutory-consortium tradition (CONOU 1982, CONAI 1997), EU waste-directive pressure transposed via D.Lgs. 22/97 ("Ronchi decree") and later consolidated under D.Lgs. 152/06, and a domestic industrial buyer (Eni's Porto Marghera biorefinery from 2014) that needed structured UCO feedstock.

Ireland already has the legal machinery to do the same. The Waste Management Act 1996 supports Producer Responsibility Initiatives (PRIs); six exist (Repak, WEEE Ireland, ELV, batteries, TRACS, farm plastics). None for cooking oil. Adding a seventh is a Statutory Instrument plus a PRI Order, same path Repak and WEEE Ireland took. Timeline historically 3 to 5 years.

The block is not technical. It is that:
1. Commercial collectors (Frylite ~22 M L/year back-collected, Olleco partnered with four Irish depots) already harvest the valuable B2B stream. Household UCO is dispersed and unprofitable.
2. Ireland's biofuel obligation (RTFO via NORA) is met by imported UCO, mostly from China and Malaysia. Recent IrBEA reporting (2024) and PQ 2025-04-01 by Richard Boyd Barrett flag widespread fraud in these imports.
3. No TD, party, or NGO has picked up the fight. No public lobbying against the idea either; just nobody making the ask.

## The peer precedent

**Spain Law 7/2022** makes household UCO separate collection mandatory from **1 January 2026**. Municipalities ("entes locales") legally required to provide collection. No pre-existing consortium tradition. This is the closest peer-country threshold-crossing event.

## CONOE specifics

- Established: Art. 47, D.Lgs. 22/1997. Now governed by Art. 233, D.Lgs. 152/2006.
- Governance: private-law consortium, public-interest mandate, supervised by Ministry of the Environment (MASE).
- Funding: per-kg environmental levy on first placers of food-grade oil on Italian market. Households do not pay directly; restaurants do not pay separately; the levy is embedded in the food-oil price.
- Mandatory participation: producers/importers, holders of waste UCO (restaurants, canteens, food processors), transporters and recyclers must join within 60 days. Fines €8,000 to €45,000. Households are NOT obligated. They are the residual gap CONOE has not closed.
- 2024 collection: 80,345 tonnes; ~98% routed to HVO/biodiesel regeneration. But still only about a quarter of total estimated national UCO generation, because the household share is mostly lost.

## Other EU comparators

- **France:** SARIA / Oleo collect commercially. No CONOE-equivalent national statutory consortium.
- **Germany:** ReFood, BRAL, Altfett-Verwertung West operate commercially under federal waste law (KrWG). Restaurant compliance enforced via fines. Household collection runs as municipal pilots only.
- **Spain:** Heading to a CONOE-tier statutory regime via Law 7/2022 from 2026.

## UK position

- UCO is not in the Simpler Recycling regulations (2025) for England.
- 2024 RTFO statistics: 77% of renewable fuel from waste feedstocks. Transport & Environment estimates ~80% of Europe's "used" cooking oil is imported, ~60% from China.
- WRAP / Recycle Now: advisory only. No statutory power to mandate council UCO collection.

## What would have to change

**Ireland**
- Mechanism: add UCO as the 7th PRI under Part V of the Waste Management Act 1996 via Statutory Instrument and Producer Responsibility Initiative Order.
- Levy: charge food-oil producers/importers a per-litre environmental contribution at first sale (mirroring CONOE's levy, applied to oil bottles on Irish supermarket shelves).
- Operator: a new private consortium, or extend an existing PRI body to cover UCO.
- Timeline: 3 to 5 years from policy consultation to operational scheme, based on Repak (1997 voluntary to 2003 mandatory) and WEEE Ireland (2003 proposed to 2005 live).

**UK**
- Add UCO to Environment Act 2021 EPR powers via DEFRA consultation and Statutory Instrument; or reform RTFO to require a domestic-collection floor for biofuel obligation accounting.
- Timeline: 3 to 6 years.
